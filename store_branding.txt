4. First-Run Onboarding Flow
To prevent user confusion regarding the 2GB+ model download, we must use a Guided Setup instead of dropping them onto an empty Home screen.
Step 1: The Privacy Promise
• Visual: Shield icon.
• Text: "Your data never leaves this phone. AINI is 100% offline, meaning no clouds, no tracking, and total privacy."

Step 2: Hardware Optimization
• Action: Background scan of RAM and Storage.
• Text: "We've analyzed your device. We recommend our [Fast 0.5B] model for the best balance of speed and battery life."
Step 3: The "Brain" Download
• Visual: Progress bar with a "Stay on Wi-Fi" warning.
• Text: "Downloading your local AI engine. This is a one-time setup to enable 24/7 access without data costs."
Step 4: Permission Request
• Action: Request Camera/Mic/Files only when explained.
• Text: "Grant access to help AINI read your documents and listen to your notes."

5. Review Improvement Strategy (The "4-Star Guard")
On-device AI on 4GB RAM devices can be slow. If we don't manage expectations, users will leave 1-star reviews for "slowness."

• Transparency in UI: Replace the generic loading spinner with a Token Counter (e.g., "Generating... 1.5 words/sec"). This shows the user the phone is working hard, turning "lag" into "process."

• The "Heating" Warning: If the phone hits thermal limits, show a polite toast: "AINI is working hard. Taking a short break to keep your device cool." This prevents "Overheated my phone" complaints.

• Proactive Feedback Loop: If a user hasn't successfully generated text in 3 tries, show a help dialog: "Running slow? Try switching to the 'Fast' model in Settings."

• The "No-Internet" Testimonial: Periodically show a small tip: "Did you know? AINI works in Airplane Mode. Try it on your next flight!" This reinforces the unique value proposition.
